Multi-word Expressions for Abusive Speech Detection in Serbian

We pay special attention to adding Multi-word expressions that can be seen as abusive, as such lexical entries are very important in obtaining good results in a plethora of abusive language detection tasks. We use Serbian morphological dictionaries as a basis for data cleaning and MWE dictionary creation. A connection to otherlexical and semantic resources in Serbian is outlined and building of abusive language detection systems based on that connection is foreseen.

Jelena Mitrović, Cliff O’Reilly, Randy Harris and Michael Granitzer

Joint Workshop on Multiword Expressions and Electronic Lexicons 2020

I feel offended, don’t be abusive! implicit/explicit messages in offensive and abusive language

In this contribution, we investigate a recent dataset for offensive language in English, namely OLID/OffensEval (Zampieri et al. 2019a; Zampieri et al. 2019b), in the light of two factors proposed by Waseem et al. 2017.

Tommaso Caselli, Valerio Basile, Jelena Mitrovic, Inga Kartoziya, Michael Granitzer

12th Conference on Language Resources and Evaluation (2020)

Cognitive Modeling in Computational Rhetoric: Litotes, Containment and the Unexcluded Middle

We extend the assessment of the Excluded Middle from the perspective of Image Schema theory into the realm of the Unexcluded Middle and draw a representation of the layout of containers and analogous concept-activation. Lastly, we create and present an OWL ontology and publish it online.

Jelena Mitrović, Cliff O’Reilly, Randy Allen Harris and Michael Granitzer

International Conference on Agents and Artificial Intelligence (2021)
